 First Thoughts

It has been truly said that the longest journey begins with the first step. This is just as true of starting (or re-starting) to read the Bible as of any other new venture we may undertake. In fact, starting (or re-starting) to read the Bible is rather like going on a journey, a journey of exploration into a new and beautiful country.

But, as with any new venture, the problem is knowing where to begin.

This page is designed to help you take that all-important first step, as well as to spur you on to take further strides forward. We are sure that once you have begun to experience the joy of reading and using the Bible for yourself, you will want to do so more and more.

Happy journeying!

 Getting Started
 How to Use This Page

We encourage you first of all to look up and write out the following references in full from your Bible. Next, summarise each one in your own words. Finally, try to say in a single word what each scripture is saying to you personally.

We have done the first as an example, but invite you to try to improve on our attempt. You may wish to print this page as a working sheet before you begin.

Click on the first reference below (Joshua 1:8) to see the verse in a number of versions.

 In Conclusion

As a result of transcribing, summarising and identifying keywords for the references we have given, we are sure you will want to know the Bible better and to read it more regularly. As you do so, you will find your Confidence in the Word is beginning to grow.

For the second stage on the journey, we invite you to undertake a similar but somewhat more extended exercise, in which you will discover that the Scriptures are Our Only Sure and Safe Foundation for your life as a disciple of the Lord Jesus Christ.
